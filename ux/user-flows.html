User Flows

UI/UX stands for User Interface and User Experience Design. These two areas of digital design are often grouped together but have profound differences. They share the same visual space when it comes to digital spaces, but the ways in which they interact with users are different.


User Experience design is focused on the overall feel of the product. The designer is in charge of finding solutions to problems that don’t have one specific right answer. User experience design is all about leading the user, step by step (also known as user flow) through the product in a cohesive, logical way.


This interaction displays the structure of a user flow, a vital, useful tool in the life of a UX Designer. User flows can vary stylistically from designer to designer, but the most important elements are to create states and decisions (rectangles and diamonds respectively) to navigate the user’s path. Each element is accompanied by a word or short phrase that sums up what the user will encounter in that step of the product.
