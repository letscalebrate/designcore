Visual Style

UI/UX stands for User Interface and User Experience Design. These two areas of digital design are often grouped together but have profound differences. They share the same visual space when it comes to digital spaces, but the ways in which they interact with users are different.


UI Design is concerned with how a product is communicated visually. In this stage, the designer is in charge of taking users through the path that has been mapped out in the user experience (UX) process. Instead of thinking about a big, cohesive picture, the designer will focus on the details of the graphical user interface (GUI).


In this interaction, the main focus is changing the visual style, which is an important part of an interface’s design. Different colors and stroke widths can fashion a user’s experience to have a certain feeling or theme.
