Color Accessibility

Color accessibility allows people with visual impairments or color vision deficiencies to interact with digital experiences the same way as non visually impaired people. Thinking about the accessibility of experiences has become an ethical practice that has been more widespread in digital design.


Some ways that design is considered accessible is by adding enough color contrast, not solely relying on color to communicate important information, and also adding contrast to focus states.


Read more about the importance of color accessibility and the WCAG compliance guidelines.
