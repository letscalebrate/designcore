Typography Fonts

Typography is the visual representation of language and therefore a large part of visual communication in design. There are many nuances to typography that take years to learn and master. One of the most basic concepts of typography is choosing the right font family.


This is similar to picking a font in Google Docs or Microsoft Word, but have you noticed the differences between the fonts? Fonts are generally categorized into 5 families. A sans serif font is a font without serifs, which are the visual accents on the end of type letters. In contrast serif fonts have serifs. Monospace fonts have the same width for each letter. Handwritten fonts are fonts designed to emulate handwriting. Lastly display fonts are generally fancier or more detailed to catch attention.
