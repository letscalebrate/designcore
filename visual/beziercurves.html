Bezier Curves

In digital design, designers use bezier curves to create vectors that the computer can interpret to create 2d vector graphics. The curve was named bézier after french mathematician Pierre Bézier who developed this method of computer drawing.


Bezier curves are made up of control points that define the vector. Designers use these points to manipulate the vector into shapes they want. Double click on the heart above to make the control points visible then try manipulating the control points.
